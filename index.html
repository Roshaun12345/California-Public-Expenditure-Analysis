California Public Expenditure Analysis

Name: Roshaun Gregory

The goal is to provide insights into the differences in public expenditure patterns across the regions of Northern California, Southern California, Central California, and the Sierra Nevada area. By tracking these patterns over the last ten years, we aim to uncover trends in spending and population dynamics.

Total Expenditure Distribution by Region (2013-2023)

More than half of California State expenditure went into Southern California, while the Sierra Nevada region received less than 1 percent of total spending.

California Regional Population Trends (2013-2023)

The population trends show relatively stable patterns over the decade with no major changes in any region. Southern California maintains the largest population, while the Sierra Nevada region has the smallest. All regions show gradual growth over the 10-year period.

California Per Capita Expenditures by Region and Year (2013-2023)

The heatmap reveals variations in per capita spending across regions and years, with some regions consistently showing higher expenditures per resident. A notable trend emerges post-2020, where values tend to be slightly higher across most regions, possibly reflecting increased public spending in response to the COVID-19 pandemic. The Central Valley region shows the most stable pattern over time, while Northern California exhibits greater year-to-year variability.

California Cities: Avg Spending vs Population (2013-2023)

While Southern California's per capita expenditure appears significantly higher than other regions, the overwhelming majority of this spending is concentrated in just two cities: Industry and Vernon. These cities have very low population estimates, which dramatically skews the per capita calculations. When excluding these outliers, Southern California's spending patterns align more closely with other regions.
